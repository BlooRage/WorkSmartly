Make

      Make is a robust visual automation tool previously known as Integromat that allows users to design, build, and automate workflows across various applications and systems without ever writing a single line of code. If you have ever wished for a simpler way to connect your tools and automate mundane tasks, Make has provided just that and more. Users can design using the drag and drop interface and map out exactly how they want their processes to run. It's like a flowchart except each step actually does something in real life. Want to move data from Google Sheets to your CRM, filter it, and send an email report? Users can perform these functions with Make applications.

      The foremost and strongest feature supported on this platform includes conditional logic, data manipulation, and realtime execution. Whenever you want to create intelligent workflows that automatically react to your actual situation, workarounds or manual updates would simply no longer be required. If you are a marketer, developer, business operator or anyone just interested in automating mundane tasks, Make gives you flexibility and control without the requirement of technical knowledge. It was built for anyone looking to create operational efficiencies and focus on what matters. It is time to find out what you can do with Make and turn your ideas into fantastic automations exactly how you want. With thousands of app integrations, the possibilities are almost endless. Start building today and experience how Make can bring your workflows to life with ease and precision.

User Interface

Make Dashboard

      Make (formerly Integromat) is characterized by a modern interface with logic and modularity at its heart. The left sidebar provides fast access to Organization, Scenarios, Templates, and Webhooks, all neatly presented under 'My Team' for collaborative team automation. Here in the center, the interface draws its users into designing scenarios where the flow of events hence suggest, 'When this happens Do this.' This loud trigger action relationship provides clarity even to those who have never wandered through its wonders. Google Sheets, Gmail, and Slack, among others, are prominently displayed with large, recognizable icons selected by the user within a heartbeat. The top navigation offers additional controls with tabs for Teams, Payments, Installed Apps, etc., keeping order in an otherwise chaotic setup. Soft gradients, friendly icons, and a vibrant yet fluent "Create scenario" button work together to create a friendly yet productive workspace.
