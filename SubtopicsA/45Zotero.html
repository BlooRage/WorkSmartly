Zotero

     Zotero is a free, open-source reference management tool developed in 2006 by the Roy Rosenzweig Center for History and New Media at George Mason University. Trusted by researchers and academics worldwide, Zotero helps users collect, organize, cite, and share research sources with ease. Unlike commercial platforms, this nonprofit-backed software is built with a strong emphasis on user rights, transparency, and academic freedom. It does not sell user data or display advertisements, ensuring that researchers can work in a secure and private environment without commercial intrusion.

      Zotero is a powerful, free, and open-source reference management tool developed and maintained by a nonprofit organization. One of its most compelling advantages is its strong commitment to user privacy and data ethics. Unlike many commercial platforms, Zotero does not harvest, sell, or exploit your personal data for advertising or profit-driven purposes. This ensures that researchers, students, and academics retain full control over their information and research activities, creating a more secure and trustworthy environment for scholarly work. Zotero also offers cloud-based syncing capabilities, enabling users to access their research libraries from multiple devices. This is particularly useful for individuals working across different locations or collaborating with peers. With group libraries, teams can collect, organize, and annotate shared resources in real time, facilitating efficient and collaborative research workflows.
